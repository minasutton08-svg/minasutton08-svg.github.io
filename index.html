Our Mission

We believe every individual deserves access to post-secondary education. No one should be forced into debt to pursue their future, especially those already burdened by the financial impact of cancer treatment. We also believe that no one should struggle to maintain stable housing or meet basic needs during remission. No one should have to question if or when they can attend college, or feel that success is out of reach. Our goal is to raise funds that empower cancer patients and their families to live stable, fulfilling, and normal lives beyond treatment.

Why It Matters

Cancer does not end when treatment does. For many, remission brings ongoing financial strain, academic disruption, and uncertainty.

Students are forced to choose between stability and education, delaying or abandoning opportunities entirely.

Did You Know?

Over 50%

of cancer patients in remission face significant financial debt due to treatment.

Who We Help

We support individuals and families impacted by cancer, ensuring that financial hardship does not prevent access to education and opportunity.

AYA Patients

Adolescents and young adults currently undergoing cancer treatment who are pursuing or planning to pursue higher education.

Survivors

Individuals in remission facing financial and academic disruption as they return to school and rebuild stability.

Families

Immediate family members financially affected by a cancer diagnosis, seeking support to continue their education.
